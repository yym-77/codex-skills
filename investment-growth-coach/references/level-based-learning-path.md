# Level-Based Learning Path

Teach by level. Avoid advanced jargon before the user needs it.

## L0 New Investor Protection

Goal: avoid large mistakes.

Teach: fund basics, net value, estimate, regular investment, position, drawdown, emergency cash.

Allow: off-exchange funds, broad index funds, small industry fund exposure.

Avoid: stocks, convertible bonds, complex ETFs, heavy theme bets.

## L1 Fund Discipline

Goal: build regular investment, position control, and review habits.

Teach: single-theme cap, extra add vs base plan, stop adding, pause regular investment, simple review.

Allow: off-exchange funds and small observation positions.

Require: daily one-sentence market note and weekly review.

## L2 Market Structure

Goal: understand market style and theme formation.

Teach: strong/weak sectors, main theme, policy, industry chain, basic K-line position, counter-evidence.

Allow: industry funds; ETF simulation only.

Require: explain why a sector is strong or weak.

## L3 ETF Practice

Goal: transition from fund timing to exchange discipline.

Teach: ETF trading rules, liquidity, bid-ask spread, premium/discount, tracking error, transaction costs.

Allow: small ETF simulation or controlled small practice after passing checks.

Require: 4 weeks review, trade reason, position cap, and invalidation condition.

## L4 Industry Research

Goal: analyze themes beyond price moves.

Teach: industry chain, supply/demand, order, price increase, earnings verification, valuation cycle.

Allow: industry ETF and active fund optimization.

Require: monthly theme research.

## L5 Stock Preparation

Goal: research companies before real stock exposure.

Teach: business model, financial statements, cash flow, margin, debt, competition, valuation, catalysts, risks.

Allow: stock simulation research.

Avoid: real heavy stock positions.

## L6 Small Stock Practice

Goal: small-position practice with strict risk control.

Teach: announcements, earnings tracking, thesis invalidation, exit discipline, position sizing.

Allow: small stock practice with strict cap.

Require: complete pre-trade thesis and post-trade review.
